# Anxiety Disorders
Group of disorders linked to excessive fear and anxiety and related maladaptive behaviours
## Symptoms
- Somatic
	- Heart racing
- Behavioural
	- Freeze
	- Flee
- Emotional
	- Dread
	- Terror
- Cognitive
	- Trouble concentrating
---
## Panic Disorder
- Unpredictable, minutes-long episodes of intense dread
- Followed by worry of next attack
- Comes in during adolescence to mid 30s
### Possible Causes
- Biological Factors
	- Genetics
	- Suffocation false alarm theory
- Cognitive Factors
	- Misperceive body sensations as potentially dangerous events
---
## Specific Phobia
- Persistent, irrational fear and avoidance of a specific object, activity, or situation
	- Animal
	- Natural environment
	- Blood, injection, and injury
	- Situational
		- Closed spaces
---
## Agoraphobia
- Intense fear or avoidance of situations in which escape might be difficult
- ---
## Possible Causes (Phobias)
- Behaviourism
	- Acquired through classical conditioning and maintained through operant conditioning
- Biological Factors
	- People are biologically prepared to develop certain fears
---
## Social Anxiety Disorder
- Intense fear and avoidance of social situations

## Generalized Anxiety Disorder
- Persistent, uncontrollable, and free-floating non-specific anxiety
- Difficulties controlling worries
- Keyed up, irritable, tense, and easily fatigued
### Possible Causes
- Diathesis-Stress
- Humanistic
- Biological Factors
- Cognitive
---
## Posttraumatic Stress Disorder
- Haunting memories
- Nightmares
- Hypervigilance
- social withdrawal
- Jump anxiety
- Numbness of feeling
- Insomnia
- Lingers for 4 weeks or more after traumatic experience
### Possible Causes
- Genetics
- Gender
- Limbic System
- ---
## Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder
- Unwanted and repetitive thoughts (obsessions), actions (compulsions), or both
### Possible Causes
- Biological Factors
	- Genetics
- Behaviourism
	- Learned an inappropriate and now vicious reinforcement cycle that is difficult to break
		- Similar to phobia acquisition

SSRIs can be effective to treat OCD

# Mood Disorders
## Major Depressive Disorder
- Emotional Symptoms
	- Depressed Mood
	- Anhedonia
- Physical Symptoms
	- Somatic Complaints
	- Motor Retardation
	- Sleep Disturbances
	- Weight Change (Must be unintentional)
- Cognitive Symptoms
	- Distorted Thinking
	- Difficulties Concentrating
---
## Bipolar Disorder
- Overexcited state of mania and usually periods of depression
- Mania
	- Emotional Symptoms
		- Inflated self-esteem
		- Elation or Irritation
	- Physical Symptoms
		- Decreased need for sleep
	- Cognitive Symptoms
		- Distractible
		- Goal-directed Behaviour
### Possible Causes
- Biological
	- Disruption of basic biological functions
	- Genetics
- Cognitive
	- Irrational Thoughts
	- Negative Self-Schemas
- Diathesis-Stress
	- Onset or relapse of depression is more likely following a major loss
---

## Personality Disorders
- Group of disorders characterized by enduring inner experiences or behaviour patterns that:
	- Differ from cultural norms and expectations
	- Pervasive and inflexible
	- Begin in adolescence or early adulthood
	- Stable over time
	- Cause distress or impairment
### Clusters
- Cluster A (Odd thinking and eccentric behaviour)
	- Paranoid
	- Schizoid
	- Schizotypal
- Cluster B (Dramatic and erratic behaviour)
	- Antisocial
	- Borderline
	- Histrionic
	- Narcissistic
- Cluster C (Severe anxiety and fear)
	- Avoidant
	- Dependant
	- Obsessive-compulsive disorder
### Possible Causes
- Temperament
- Diathesis-Stress Model
	- Biological Predisposition
		- Genetics (Cluster B)
		- Epigenetics
		- Hormones
		- Neurotransmitters
		- Abnormal central nervous system functioning
	- Psychosocial Factors
		- Childhood trauma
		- Disturbances in early attachment formation
		- Peer relationships
### Treatments
- No standard treatments
- Goal is to address various specific symptoms
- Focus on reducing interpersonal conflict and stabilizing psychosocial functioning
---
## Antisocial Personality Disorder
- Disregard and violation of the rights of others
	- Failure to conform to social norms
	- Deceitfulness
	- Impulsivity
	- Irritability and aggressiveness
	- Reckless disregard for safety of self and others
	- Irresponsibility
	- Lack of remorse
- 2-3% of the population
	- Higher rates in men compared to women
- Psychopathy?
---
## Borderline Personality Disorder
- Instability in mood, self-image and interpersonal relationships
	- Marked impulsivity
	- rapidly shifting emotions
	- Distorted perception of self and others
	- Frantic efforts to avoid abandonment
	- Chronic feelings of emptiness
- 0.7-2.7% of the population
	- Higher rates in women
---
## Narcissistic Personality Disorder
- Grandiose
- Need for admiration
- Lack of empathy
	- Preoccupied with fantasies of success, power, beauty, idealization
	- Belief of being "special"
	- Sense of entitlement
	- Exploitative
	- Envious of others, or believe others are envious of them
	- Arrogant
- 1-2% of the population
	- Higher rates in men
