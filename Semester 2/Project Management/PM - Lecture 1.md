# Project Life Cycle

## Phases of a project
### Initiation
- Sponsor identified
- Scope defined
- Budget approved
- Broad statements made about risk, approach, timelines
- Stakeholders identified
- Project Manager assigned
### Planning
- Deliverables Defined
- Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) defines the "work packages"
- The work packages are used to develop a schedule
- Plans are made for management of:
	- stakeholders
		- Impacted by success of project
		- Include project sponsor /team
		- may include customers, suppliers, vendors, etc.
		- stakeholder register
	- communication quality
		- Within the team
		- Outside of the team
		- Communication plan
			- Who
			- What
			- Frequency
			- Who's responsible
	- costs
		- Develop budget
		- plan cash flow
		- track expenses
		- explain deviations
	- risks
		- Likelihood of an event that would negatively or positively affect the project
	- procurement
		- Contracting to obtain supplies/services
		- Could be complex
		- Time schedule is crucial
		- Almost always requires additional experts such as lawyers
	- resources
		- Human resources
		- selection/development of project team
		- motivation and management of project team
### Implementation
- Project team is hired and developed
- Procurement takes place
- work is underway
- project to be monitored and changes made
- communication to stakeholders is important
### Closing
- Reporting
- Closing out procurements
- Identify lessons learned
- Celebrate

# Project Management Methodologies
Guiding principles and procedures for managing a project
- Standardisation
- structure
- outcomes are reproducible
- learn from previous errors
- ![[Pasted image 20260223113647.png]]

## Advantages of Methodologies
- Communication flow
- Control management
- Global Competence
- Providing Support during uncertain times
- Mapping Processes to Success

## Disadvantages of Methodologies
- Not flowless
- Many different routes/strategies
- Disconnection between project managers values and organisation's beliefs to be beneficial
- Lack of value seen in experts knowledge in subject and what they bring to the table

## PM Methodology Groups
- Uncertainty Methodologies
	- Event Chain Methodology (ECM) Uncertainty models and optimizing schedules
	- Extreme Project Management (XPM) Managing stakeholders and eliminating uncertainty via efficient collaboration
- PRINCE2: 7 Principles, 7 Themes, 7 Processes
	- Lean Project Management: maximum efficiency, maximum waste
	- Six Sigma: Improvement by eliminating defects
	- Lean Six Sigma: No waste + 0 defects
- Traditional, Sequential Methodologies
	- Waterfall: Task A, Task B, Task C
	- Critical Path Methods: String dependent tasks together
	- Critical Chain Project Management: Reserve resources for critical tasks
- The Agile Family
	- Scrum: Sprints, Clearing roadblocks
	- Kanban: Tasks made visual in lanes
	- Extreme Programming: Short work sprints, frequent iterations
	- Adaptive Project Framework: Using Requirements Breakdown Structure (RBS) to define goals, Stakeholders can change scope at each point
- Other Methodologies
	- PRiSM: Managing the eco-friendly way
	- Benefits Realisation: Delivering the benefits the customer expects
- Soft Systems Methodologies
	- A system approach integrating all parts of the project situation to problem-solve with a holistic approach
