## Projective Tests
Responses to ambiguous stimuli reveal aspects of a person's personality
- Rorschach Inkblot Test
	- Used to assess:
		- Personality structure
		- dynamics
		- presence or absence of a thought disorder
		- accuracy of a reality testing
- Thematic Apperception Test
	- Asked to tell a dynamic story as they can for a picture
	- Evaluates Psychodynamics 
	- Offender may also create non-revealing answers

## Other Tests
Personality Inventories
- Highly standardized, objective tests
- Typically assess personality traits in a general way
- Used to evaluate specific disorders (psychopathy)

Intellectual and Cognitive Evaluation
- Evaluate intellectual and cognitive levels
- May have far reaching implications regarding competency and criminal responsibility
	- Example: Contract killer evaded police for 30 years due to high social comprehension and practical reasoning, but overall intelligence was very low

# Applications of Behavioural Sciences
1. **Uncovering Deceptive Behaviour**
	- ==Distortion or exaggeration== of symptoms commonly occur
	- Malingering
		- ==Choose "trendy" illness 
		- ==Exaggerate symptoms
		- ==Difficulty to maintain consistency of symptoms==
	- Dissimulation
		- Attempt to minimize or deny mental disorder
		- Done to get released from prison or hospital

2. **Competency to Stand Trial**
	- Primary concern in criminal matters
	- A defendant must be ==free from mental illness that impacts== his/her ability to know what is happening in his/her case
	- Important factors
		- ==Nature of charges
		- ==Consequences of the charges
		- ==Individual's understanding of courtroom procedure
	- If deemed incompetent they will be hospitalized

3. **Legal Insanity**
	- A person won't be found guilty by reason of insanity if they don't comprehend the criminality of their actions
	- Psychiatrist can provide opinion as to the ==mental illness and effect== it has on a person but not an opinion on ==insanity==
	- Crime consists of two parts:
		- The guilty act (actus reus)
		- The guilty mind/intent (mens rea)
	- Legal insanity removes the mens rea component

4. **Diminished Capacity**
	- Has different meanings where you go
	- Had diminished ability to meet all the criteria for charges against him/her
		- Could not for ==specific intent
		- could not act in a ==knowing and purposeful manner
	- May be a complete defense resulting in the acquittal of the charges

5. **Dealing with Sex Offenders**
	- Sexual Psychopath Laws
		- Mandated the evaluation of treatment of sex offenders 
		- specialized treatment programs
		- Continue to commit offenses when released
		- Untreatable

6. **Post-Trial Sentencing**
	- Behavioural Scientists
		- Called to give opinions on sentencing
		- Psychiatric testimony where medication is required
	- Death Penalty
		- Psychiatrist testifies whether the defendant is competent to be sentenced to death or executed at a later date
