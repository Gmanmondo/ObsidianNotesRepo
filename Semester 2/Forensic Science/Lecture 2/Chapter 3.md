# Common types of Physical evidence
- Blood, sweat, semen, saliva
- Documents
- Drugs
- Explosives
- Fibers
- Fingerprints
- Firearms and ammunition
- Glass
- Hair
- Impressions
- Organs and physiological fluids
- Paint
- Petroleum products
- Plastic bags
- Plastic, rubber, and other polymers
- Powder residues
- Serial numbers
- Soil and minerals
- Tool marks
- Vehicle lights
- Wood and other vegetative matter

---
If you have an "overwhelming" amount of classical evidence, it can individualize and thus lead to a one person

# Forensic Databases
## Fingerprint Databases
- Integrated Automated Fingerprint Identification System (IAFIS)
- Maintained by the FBI
- Each state has its own Automated Fingerprint Identification System (AFIS)

## DNA Databases
- Combines DNA Index System (CODIS)
- National DNA Data Bank, in Ottawa
	- 379,000 DNA profiles from convicted individuals

## Genealogy Databases
- Used to identify close relatives
- 23andMe
- Ancestry.com
- Used to narrow search to a family tree

## Other Databases
### National Integrated Ballistics Information Network (NIBIN)
- Maintained by Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives
- Can process markings made by firearm bullets and cartridge casings

### International Forensic Automotive Paint Data Query (PDQ)
- Chemical and color information pertaining to original automotive paints
- Maintained by RCMP

### Shoe Prints (SICAR)
- 8,500 Entries
- Maintained by RCMP
- Can match print from a picture or scan
