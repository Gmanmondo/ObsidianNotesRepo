The study of insects and other arthropods associated with legal issues

Criminal and civil cases.

	- Must pass 80% or over written in 12hr exam

## Flies
Different Behaviours
- Bluebottle & Greenbottle
	- Primary colonisers
- Flesh Fly
	- Not primary Colonisers
- Cheese Skipper
	- Tiny
	- Wait till body is in second phase of decomposition
	- Curl up into tiny ball then pop like popcorn
- Coffin Fly
	- Smaller than fruit flies
	- Lay eggs on buried remains
	- Will dig down in shallow burials

# Stages of Decomposition
## Fresh
- Right after death
- We couldn't tell if organism is dead
- Flies can tell immediately
## Bloat
- Gasses collect and swell the body
- Bacteria create more gas
- Obvious order
## Active
- Recognisable decay of the carcass
- Lots of insect activity
- Putrid odor
## Advanced
- Large amount of flesh removed
- moist tissue still present
- Odour is still noticeable and bad but not as bad as before
## Dry/Remains
- Reduced to bones
- Cartilage
- Exudate
- Dry skin
- Some insects still present
- Slight odour still present

# Post-mortem Interval (PMI)
- Entire period after death
- Pre and post colonization

## Min PMI
- When the body is first colonized

## Determining minimum post mortem interval
### Method 1: Insect Development
- Aging the oldest immature insects
	- Hours, days, weeks
### Method 2: Insect succession
- Successional colonization of insects present on the body
	- Weeks, months -> Year+
