# Attitudes

## Routes to Persuasion
Dual processes model says that there are two pathways to persuading others
(Equally effective)
### Central Route
- Focuses on informational content
- Persuasion can be more long lasting
### Peripheral Route
- Focuses on surface aspects of the argument
- Focus on topic is short term

---
## Persuasion Tactics
### Door-in-the-Face
- Large request first
- Smaller request second

### Foot-in-the-Door
- Small request first
- Larger request second

## Cognitive Dissonance Theory
- We act to reduce the discomfort that we feel when there's a lack of consistency between two of our thoughts or one of our thoughts and behaviours.
	- Free choice
	- Knowledge of consequences

### Insufficient Justification
- People act in an attitude-discrepant way without receiving a large reward
![[Pasted image 20251209115612.png]]
### Justifying Effort
- We alter our attitudes to justify our suffering

### Justifying Difficult Decisions
- Exaggerate the positives of what you chose and exaggerate the negatives of what you didn't
- Long lasting

# Social Influence
## Conformity
- Change of behaviour to coincide with a group

### Sherif (1935)
- Participants in dark room were shown a light and estimated the distance the light moved
- Repeated in 3 group sessions.
 ![[Pasted image 20251209120311.png]]

### Asch (1955)
- Participants were asked to select the line closest in length to X
- When When confederates first gave obviously wrong answers, more than 1/3 of participants conformed.
![[Pasted image 20251209120651.png]]

### Why Do We Conform?
- Norms
	- Normative Social Influence
		- Conformity motivated by a desire to gain approval or avoid disapproval
	
	- Informational Social Influence
		- Conformity motivated by the belief that others are correct
## Obedience
- Change of behaviour due to a direct command

### Milgram's Obedience Experiments
- Memory Study
	- Teacher and Learner
	- Teacher gives a learner increasingly intense shocks if he answers incorrectly
	- When will people stop administering shocks?

- Most people will obey the experimenter and continue to administer shocks

## Social Loafing
- Tendency to do less when you work as a group toward a common goal as opposed to when you're judged individually

## Problems with Group-Decision Making
### Group Polarization
- The enhancement of a group's prevailing inclinations through discussion within the group
- People tend to move more toward one way of thinking after a group chat
### Groupthink
- Desire for harmony in a decision-making group overrides a realistic appraisal of alternatives

## The Bystander Effect
- The tendency for any given bystander to be less likely to give aid if others are present

## Why People Help
### Social Exchange Theory
- Social behaviour is an exchange process, maximize benefits and minimize costs

### Social Norms
- Reciprocity Norms
	- Expectation that people will help, not hurt, those who have helped them

- Social-responsibility norm
	- Expectation that people will help those needing their help
